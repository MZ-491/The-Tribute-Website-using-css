Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan

1936-2021

Proud Of Pakistan

Abdul Qadeer Khan, NI, HI, FPAS (/ˈɑːbdəl ˈkɑːdɪər ˈkɑːn/ ⓘ AHB-dəl KAH-deer KAHN; Urdu: عبد القدیر خان; 1 April 1936 – 10 October 2021),[3] known as A. Q. Khan, was a Pakistani nuclear physicist and metallurgical engineer who is colloquially known as the "father of Pakistan's atomic weapons program".[a]Abdul Qadeer Khan was a Pakistani engineer, a key figure in Pakistan's nuclear weapons program.Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan is credited with inventing gas-centrifuge enrichment technology for Pakistan's nuclear program.

Award

Dr. Khan received Pakistan’s highest civilian awards, including the Nishan-e-Imtiaz (twice) and Hilal-e-Imtiaz, recognizing his significant contributions to Pakistan’s defense capabilities.
